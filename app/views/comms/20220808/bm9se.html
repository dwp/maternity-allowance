What to do now 

Wait for your letter from HMRC which will be with you within 10 working days from the date of this letter. Please do not contact HMRC unless you have not received it within that time.

Once you have received your letter from HMRC and paid the NI Contributions, we will look at your MA again.

If you have a Budget Payment Plan with HMRC  

HMRC may allocate money you have paid into your Budget Payment Plan towards your Class 2 contributions record. If this happens, HMRC will contact you.

While you are getting MA

  We will usually credit your NI account with contributions for each week you are getting MA. These contributions are called credits. They may help you get State Pension and other social security benefits in the future.

 

If you are self-employed, you do not have to pay the self-employed NI contributions for each week you are getting MA. 

A week runs from Sunday to Saturday.

Changes you should tell us about

Please tell us if your baby is born more than 11 weeks before the baby is due. You will still be able to get the same amount of MA, but you will get it from the day after your baby is born.

Please tell us if your baby is born less than 11 weeks before the baby is due. If your baby is born

  • less than 11 weeks before the baby is due, and
  • before your MA Period has started
 

You will still get the same amount of MA. But you will get it from the day after your baby is born.

Working in your MA period

You will be able to work as an employed or self-employed person for up to 10 days without losing any MA. These days are called ‘Keeping in Touch’ days and will help you stay in touch with your employer or with your business without losing your MA. Any amount of work that you do in a day will be counted as one ‘day’, even if you work just an hour or half a day.

You must tell us about any work you do as an employed or self-employed person, including Keeping in Touch days.

Shared Parental Leave and Statutory Shared Parental Pay

You and your partner or the other parent of the child may be able to get Shared Parental Leave or Statutory Shared Parental Pay after the baby is born.

If you are eligible and decide to take Shared Parental Leave or Statutory Shared Parental Pay, you must give us at least 8 weeks notice that you want to end your MA early, unless you have already returned to work. If you have already returned to work, your MA will end in the week in which your notice is given. The end date must be the last date of your benefit week. For example, if your MA began on a Tuesday, the benefit week will end on a Monday.

For more information go to www.gov.uk/shared-parental-leave-and-pay

If you are sick

If you were sick before your MA began, or if you are still sick when your MA ends, you may be able to get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) from Jobcentre Plus.

You cannot get ESA while you are getting MA.

Other changes you must tell us about

You or someone who acts for you must also tell us if you:

  • do any work as an employed or self-employed person
  • take part in the business of your self-employed spouse or civil partner
  • go abroad outside the European Economic Area or Switzerland
  • go to the Isle of Man or Channel Isles and want to get your money while there
  • go into prison or are held in legal custody. By legal custody we mean being in prison or being held by the police
  • claim or get Statutory Adoption Pay
  • move to a new address
  • start to get an occupational pension from an employer or get an increase in an occupational pension
  • or your spouse, civil partner or anyone else claims any money for you or gets money added to their benefit for you

Your representative must also contact us if you die.

If any of these things happen, or any other changes you think we should know about happen, please tell us straightaway. Please tell us about the change and when it happened.

If you do not tell us about a change and we pay you too much MA we have the right to take back that money.

We will contact you before we take back any money.

If you disagree with a decision

You can ask us to explain why

You, or someone who has the authority to act for you, can phone or write to us within one month of the date on this letter to ask us to explain our decision in writing.

You can also ask us to reconsider a decision

Tell us if you have more information, or if you think we have overlooked something which might change the decision. Do this within one month of the date on this letter.

We will look at what you tell us and send you a letter to tell you what we have decided, and why. We call this letter a Mandatory Reconsideration Notice.

When you have done this you can appeal

If you disagree with the Mandatory Reconsideration Notice, you can appeal to a tribunal.

You must wait for the Mandatory Reconsideration Notice before you start an appeal.
